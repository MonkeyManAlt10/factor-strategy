IS TOP-10'S DRAWDOWN MEANINGFULLY WORSE?
-----------------------------------------
Yes. The gap is material:

1. TOP-10 HITS -32.7%, TOP-50 HITS -21.6% — an 11 pp difference.
   That's comparable to the gap between top-50 and a typical 2x leveraged ETF.
   In dollar terms on a $100K portfolio: top-10 peak-to-trough loss is $32,700
   vs $21,600 for top-50.

2. TOP-50 IS ACTUALLY SHALLOWER THAN SPY (-21.6% vs -23.9%). The diversification
   of 50 equal-weight positions dampens drawdowns below the index level, which is
   a real advantage.

3. TOP-10 EXCEEDS SPY's DRAWDOWN BY 8.75 PP. It takes on more market risk (beta 1.10)
   AND more concentration risk. During momentum crashes (2020, 2022), concentrated
   bets in high-momentum names blow up faster and deeper than a diversified book.

4. WHY THE GAP EXISTS: Top-10 holds ~20% in each of its top 2 names. If momentum
   reverses in two or three concentrated positions simultaneously (as happened in
   2022 with tech), you hit severe drawdowns with no diversification cushion.

PRACTICAL IMPLICATION:
Top-10 requires ~50% more drawdown tolerance than top-50 for a roughly 40% higher
expected CAGR. Whether that trade-off is worth it is a live question we're testing
going forward — but the in-sample numbers suggest it is NOT worth it on a Sharpe
basis (1.09 vs 1.15), only on a raw return basis.
